Hyperbola from 2 asymptotes and 1 curve point    

This command creates a hyperbola that has two given straight lines as asymptotes and runs through a given third point.

First click on the two straight lines that are to serve as asymptotes, and then click the point the hyperbola is to run through. Finally, an appropriate hyperbola is created and displayed.

    To get a "right" hyperbola the two asymptotic lines must intersect, and the curve point must not lie on any of the asymptotes. If one of these two conditions is not met, you will not get a valid hyperbola. In some cases, you may get a degenerated hyperbola, but DynaGeo will not show this. Instead, the hyperbola is internally marked as invalid and not displayed at all.
