Arc    

(automatically repeatable)

An arc is determined by 3 points:

  1. the starting point of the arc,

  2. the midpoint of the circle containing the arc and

  3. a third point to state where the arc should end: it will run until it reaches the ray that starts in the midpoint and runs through this third point.

Arcs are always drawn with mathematically positive orientation.


Please note:

    You cannot reflect an arc in a line just by mirroring the 3 defining points and then create the appropriate arc depending on the mirrored points. This will lead to wrong results due to the fact that mirroring at a line reverses the orientation. You better use the command "Reflect an object in a line".
