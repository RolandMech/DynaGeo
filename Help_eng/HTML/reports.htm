Reports from the programmer's workshop


    The problem of the "Jumping points"

      This small article shows a classical problem of "Dynamic geometry systems" that has kept the DGS programmers busy over a couple of years. In the meantime we've learned that the problem is unsolvable.


    The problem of the intersection

      In this section you learn about a very basic problem of the classical geometry that leads to unexpectedly difficult questions when you produce your drawings with a DGS and therefore they become "moveable".


    The problem of the recursive functions

      Here you get a little introduction to the difficulties you will be faced with when you want to draw the graphs of recursive functions with a DGS like DynaGeo.


    The problem of the angle unit

      Some background information how DynaGeo measures angle widths. A detailed example shows you how to get along with the mess the mathematicians caused by not deciding the struggle between "deg" and "rad".
