Measure distance    

(automatically repeatable)

With the left mouse button, click on the two objects you want to measure the distance between. You can choose any combination of points, lines and circles, and the objects may be selected in any order.

If the distance between the selected objects is positive, a so-called "line of distance" is constructed and displayed. Its length is shown in a small frame. For two intersecting lines and/or circles no line of distance is constructed. Their distance is assumed to be zero.

You may drag the display frame with the mouse.


Please note:

  • If you want to measure the distance between a line segment and another object, internally EUKLID DynaGeo uses the (long) line including this segment. It is thereby easier to e.g. measure the perpendicular in all triangles.

  • For all other combinations of points, lines and circles a correct topological concept of distance is used: The distance between two sets of points is the shortest of all distances between points of one set to points of the other set.



ALT variant: Easy measuring of segment lengths

    If you start this command by clicking the tool button on the Measure & Calculate bar, and you hold down the ALT key while you click, then only one parameter will be expected: when you click on a segment, the length of this segment will be measured and displayed.
