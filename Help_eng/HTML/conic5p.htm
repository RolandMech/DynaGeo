General conic through 5 points    

This command creates a general conic running through 5 given points. Depending on the mutual position of these 5 points, you can get an ellipse, an hyperbola, a parabola or an degenerated conic.

First click 5 different points, one after the other. After the last click the conic is calculated and will be shown.


Please notice:

    In order to get at most one conic, the 5 points must be pairwise distinct. If there really exists a conic through all 5 points depends on the mutual position of the points. The following cases are possible:

    • You get an not-degenerated conic, i.e. an ellipse, a hyperbola or a parabola, if every straight line through 2 of the 5 points does not run through any of the 3 other points (i.e.: no 3 points out of the 5 are collinear).

    • If there is a straight line that runs through exact 3 of the 5 defining points, then you get a degenerated conic, e.g. a pair of straight lines.

    • If there is a straight line that runs through at least 4 of these points, then there may be no solution oder more than one solution. When this happens, DynaGeo shows no conic. If this happens during the dragging of the drawing, an existing conic will become invalid and is wiped from the window.
